Our Principals

  • Respect

  • Cooperation

  • Equality

  • Growth

  • Self Driven Improvement

Here in Crumlin Youthreach, we believe that all of our Learners are capable of achieving great things, no matter their background. We aim to encourage this with a wide variety of classes available, to ensure that everyone has the chance to explore what they would like to do when they graduate.

To achieve this, we have built an education environment that fosters a sense of community and respect above all else. By doing this we hope that every new Learner is able to find a comfortable and supportive environment when they walk through our doors.

Our Connections

As we continue to improve on the modules we offer, we are always making new connections with school, colleges, universities and business around Ireland!

  • Microsoft

For the last two years we have taken part in Microsoft's STEM Passport initiative, giving our Learners a chance to learn directly from one of the world's biggest tech companies. We have also begun to take part in their Dream Space Ambassador programme, where our Learners have been furthering their skills and even passing this knowledge on to their fellow Learners and their community.

  • FIT - Fastrack into Information Technology

Since September of 2024 we have been integrating FIT's iDEA Badges into our IT Skills coursework as a way of giving our Learners a broader view of Information Technology, and a chance to explore new topics that might interest them.

What we offer

Here in Crumlin Youthreach we offer QQI Level 4 certifications for Early School Leavers between the ages of 16 and 20.

Over their two year course Learners will cover topics aimed at both personal and real world development, including;

  • Catering

  • Communications & Interculturalism

  • Health Related Fitness

  • Information Technology Skills

  • Maths

  • SPHE

  • STEAM & 3D Printing

  • Woodworking

  • Work Experience

  • Workplace Safety

  • Health Related Fitness

  • Personal & Interpersonal Development

As well as a number of extracurricular opportunities.

Cooked meals are provided every day through the Catering course, where each group of Learners has a day to cook for the entire centre.

Students receive a weekly pay packet based on their attendance as part of Social Welfare. The amount differs based on age, and hours attended in the centre.
